You wake them up.

You let them sleep.

You let out a loud squeak, waking your family. Your siblings stretch, yawn, and get ready to play for the day. Your mother, on the other hand, seems quite unhappy that you so abruptly woke her. In attempts to calm you down, she licks your head.

You squirm away.

A morning head rub. Nice!

Heck, mom's asleep. She won't notice.

Mom probably gave that advice for a reason.

You sneak out when no one's looking.

Mom probably gave that advice for a reason.

With a wet head you follow your sibling to the food bowl. They had already started without you. By the time you finished eating, they were long gone, playing in a different room. Then you notice the cat door leading outside.

Nobody's around to see you. Why not?

There's probably a good reason not to go outside.

Pushing through the cat door, you step outside for the first time in your life. The morning mist has begun to burn away and the sun is drying the dew from the grass. This was a great idea. You continue walking until the grass meets a large dark forest.

You decide to go into the forest.

The forest is pretty scary. Perhaps not.

You enter the dark forest. After wandering for a bit you discover a large fast river. You wonder what's on the other side.

You decide to swim across the river.

You think against it. The water is moving pretty fast.

You play in the yard for a few hours chasing butterflies and leaves. Then you notice a large road at the end of the grass. But you begin to think about your family back inside.

You decide to go back inside.

The road looks fun to cross!

Following the edge of the river you come to an edge of the edge of the forest. You see a wide paved road.

The cars look really scary. It's probably too dangerous to cross.

The other side of the road is filled with houses. Perhaps one of them is yours.

You watch the road carefully, making sure there are no cars before you cross. When the moment comes, you sprint as fast as you can across the pavement. Once safely on the other side, you realize none of these houses smell like your own.

Lonley, you begin to cry loudly for your family.

You wanter from house to house.

Each house begins to look like the house before it. You start to miss your family and wish you had never left home. You smell food coming from the next house. Hungry you follow the smell. You discover a large bowl of cat food on the porch. After stuffing your face you realize there is a cat door next to the front door.

Keep looking for your home.

Go inside the cat door.

A strange human finds you. It is tall and scary. But it bends down and makes clicking noises with its mouth while holding out a hand.

Go closer to the human. It seems gentile.

Don't trust the large human and keep running.

You then continue the rest of the day playing inside like a normal kitten. At the end of the day you fall asleep with your family.

Tired after a long day in the forest, you find your way back home. You make your way back inside the kitty door. Your mother and siblings rush up to you.
